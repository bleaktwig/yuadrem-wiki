    Languages

    There are currently 16 recognized languages spoken in the Fesh Peninsula, most of which have many dialects. They are split into two distinct sections: omietian and uldian language trees.

    A comprehensive overlook of the languages spoken in the Fesh Peninsula, 798 A.S.. The branches represent the divergence of families, while the leaf clusters are the languages currently spoken. The size of each leaf cluster is a rough approximation of the number of speakers by the year of production. Recorded by the Fremite Monastery of Chist.

    Omietian languages are those that were taught by Et, Om, and Tol to their respective kins: humes, imates, and fey. While etian and omian branches were taught by different god-trees, they share many common roots, leading to the theory that they sprung from one original tongue.

    Etian branch

    Jantherlin is the earliest known language that is still spoken. It was taught by Et to the first humes, and has been carefully preserved over the years by monks and scholars. In modern times, jantherlin is only spoken in academic contexts, and its writing system is the base of the etian apomantic system.

    Avshenesian family

    Over time, jantherlin evolved into most of the languages spoken in Yuadrem, the largest branch of which is the avshenesian family. Originally spoken in the Seven Princedoms, it spread far into the continent, only for the different tongues to coalesce again into the Fesh Peninsula. The avshenesian apomantic system started from avshenese, the mother language of the family, and is the most commonly used apomantic system thanks to its simplicity and similitude to frisian.

    Frisian was spoken in the peninsula before the Penance, and remains the most common vocabulary in the region. It is currently spoken in the sumis of Fremit, Ushpavam, Vamerit, and Xarxage, as well as in the Holy Princedom of Ushpavam and the Kingdom of Meritan. A dialect of old Avshenesian, analogous to frisian, is also spoken by the chiefdoms of Raraspan and Dzarog, as well as by the Ketepi people.

    Shanise was the main language spoken in the Northern Territories, and it branched out from avshenese in the thulk side of the Ird Diaspora. As of today, it is only spoken in the Kingdom of Gronselar. Shanise is the basis for the gronselarian apomantic system.

    Voskian is a family of dialects that developed north of the Zoedrem desert, with the dispersion of the bughna to the north. It is not currently spoken in any chiefdom, sumi, or kingdom, but the family is frequent in the six thousand tribes and the peoples of Saefel.

    Beast Speech is an artificial vocabulary forged in Voskferm, a kingdom that disappeared in the Penance. The language was designed to communicate with the highly intelligent Anthutes, large horned mammals that were once common in that region. It is now used by merchants and travelers to ride these animals across the sumis and kingdoms of the Fesh Peninsula.

    Harualian family

    Some time before the avshenesian family sprouted from jantherlin, the harualian family started taking shape after the hairus, kulbaba, and dral sides of the Ird Diaspora. While many harualian languages existed in the old world, only two are known to have survived the Penance.

    Kualinese was spoken in the Jenkashian empire. While the empire was lost to nix, some of its citizens migrated to and established in the Fesh Peninsula. The language is now spoken by the Puneui people, and is the second most common in the sumi of Fremit.

    Zsekian defined the Hulnar empire. Only a smatter of native speakers remain in the modern world, but the tongue still sees some use in the chiefdoms of Raraspan and Dzarog, as well as by the Ketepi people to the west.

    Shamabic family

    After the god-tree Tol spawned from the corpse of Et, it spawned the fay kin. The fey quickly integrated into the Seven Princedoms, but cultivated their own vocabularies directly from jantherlin: the shamabic family.

    Fruenese was the first language made by the fey. It is highly practical and has a straightforward syntax, reflecting the pragmatism of the kin. Fruenese is now the main tongue spoken in the chiefdom of Nalash and the island of Trevit.

    The Silent Tongue was the second language developed by the fey. It doesn't have a writing or oral system, as it uses only visual-manual signs to convey meaning. Despite it being artificial, it has split into many different dialects over the years. It is spoken in the entirety of the Fesh Peninsula, but is seen the most in the sumi of Fremit. It is the basis for the dentralin apomantic system.

    The True Tongue was the third and final language in the shamabic family. It's heavily based on fruenese, but it adds to it a large set of unique and sophisticated grammatical rules. These make it exceedingly hard to learn, but give it a unique advantage: it is impossible to lie in this tongue, hence its name. True Tongue is most commonly spoken by scholars and monks.

    Omian branch

    Babazano was taught to the imates by Om, and is the only language in the marsetian family. It is repetitive and has little verb variety, making it easy to learn. It consists of only 10 consonants and 4 vowels, turning lip reading into an effortless skill to acquire for its speakers. Currently, babazano is the only tongue spoken in the Kingdom of Om, and is spoken by the many nomad imates of the peninsula.

    Naenkian family

    Since Tek talks through its root system and spores, the god-tree did not teach any tongue to the elves. They were, however, later taught verbal language by the imates, establishing communication between the two kins. Elves learned a modified version of babazano adapted to their unique morphology, which turned into the naenkian family.

    Gannagian, sometimes referred to as old knaenese, is the first tongue forked from babazano. Lacking teeth and lips, elves communicate only with their alveolar ridge and palate, and as such gannagian words have rugged sounds, and are hard to learn for members of the other kins. The language is only spoken in Gannag, and is the basis for the gannagian apomantic system.

    Knaenese is the most common tongue in the naenkian family, and is spoken in the chiefdom of Knagan. The elves there, unable to rely on Tek's roots and spores, had to develop their language to something much more complex than the simple Gannag.

    Leaf Runes is a script imagined by imates and elves that was used to communicate between the two. The words are written into trees, and the shape of each glyph depends on the texture of their bark. This makes it challenging for the other kins to understand the writing, but is fairly simple for the imates and elves thanks to their intimate relation with trees. Leaf Runes are mostly used to provide secretive guidance in forests.

    Uldian tree

    Under the tutelage of the god-tree Uld, the fey of Ignelli invented the uldian tree of languages. These didn't spread far from the Everwoods, but managed to survive the Penance with the fey that migrated into the Fesh Peninsula.

    Odhualen is a unique tongue, sharing almost no characteristic with the other dialects of Yuadrem. It is the main language spoken in the island-state of Mbeat, and it has also recently gained popularity among scholars due to the conciseness of its writing system.

    Krelho was a variation of odhualen as it spread across the Wildlands. It has almost disappeared after the Penance, and finding native speakers is almost impossible.
